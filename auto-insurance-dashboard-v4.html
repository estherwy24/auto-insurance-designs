What this state proves: The auto tab isn't just a marketplace anymore. It's a cross-product agent: PFM detects the cash gap, Trellis confirms the bill, the Garage holds vehicle context, Instacash and Split are right there as solutions, and the long-term match still sits below — sequenced behind today's payment because Ali can't switch what she can't pay.

The progression

State 1 → 2 → 3 is the same surface getting smarter. Not three different screens — one screen with progressively more data and progressively more product working together. That's the pitch in one image.

What State 3 unlocks (that State 2 can't)

  • Trellis live policy: coverage type, deductible, full renewal terms — beyond what we can derive from RoarMoney transactions alone
  • Auto Garage: 2019 Honda Civic Sport, plate, mileage, KBB value, service interval. Vehicle becomes a first-class object in MoneyLion, not just an insurance abstraction
  • Cash flow trigger: RoarMoney sees $186 balance, knows $310 hits in 4 days. PFM detects the gap. Smart action surfaces Instacash ($124 now) or Split ($77.50 × 4) right when relevant
  • Sequenced advice: Top match card explicitly defers — "Cover Apr 15 first. Switch can wait until Sep 15." MoneyLion isn't pushing a switch when there's a more urgent cash flow problem to solve

The Smart Action card anatomy

Eyebrow with pulsing dot uses Yellow-500 (warning, not red — this is heads-up, not crisis). Three-stat row (Due / Balance / Short) shows the math at a glance, with the shortfall highlighted in Red-600. Two action buttons side-by-side: Instacash (teal — primary positive cash flow) and Split (black — neutral premium alternative). Both are MoneyLion products — no third-party leakage at the moment of need.

The card sits on a darker hero overlay (heavier than States 1-2) because the action card is the message. The photo is atmosphere only.

[System addition] flags

  • Smart Action card pattern. Three-stat math row + two MoneyLion-product action buttons + alert eyebrow. This is a reusable surface for any cash flow gap moment (rent, utilities, subscriptions). Worth promoting to MLDS as "Cash Flow Action Card".
  • Auto Garage card. Vehicle as a first-class entity. Pattern likely extends to Loans (auto loan), Marketplace (warranty, repair), and PFM (depreciation tracking).
  • Trellis-verified tag. Source-of-truth indicator that distinguishes derived data (transactions) from authoritative data (insurer API). Same anatomy could apply to Plaid-verified, IRS-verified, employer-verified data points.

Proactive insight

The Smart Action card's logic should govern hero priority across the whole app. When there's no cash flow gap, the savings-opportunity hero (State 2) leads. When there is, the Smart Action hero (State 3) takes over automatically. Same surface, different priority based on what's actually urgent for the user that day. This is the personalization engine doing its real job — choosing which message gets the hero slot.

One thing to validate [Hypothesis — worth testing]

That offering Instacash specifically tied to a known bill ("$124 to cover State Farm Apr 15") converts higher than offering Instacash generically ("Need cash? Get up to $300"). Hypothesis: contextual triggers more than triple intent. Easiest test: A/B the standard Instacash entry point against a bill-specific entry point in the Cashflow Hub. Measure tap-through and downstream repayment health.

One risk to flag

Three states stacked is the design narrative. Production reality: each unlock requires real product work — Trellis integration scope, Garage data model, RoarMoney → marketplace event bus, Instacash/Split eligibility checks at quote time. The screen is the cheapest part of State 3. If we ship the design before the data pipes, the dashboard will look generic again. Worth scoping the data layer alongside the visual.
