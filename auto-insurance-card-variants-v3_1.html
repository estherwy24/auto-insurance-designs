Layout differences

Layout 1 — Split Editorial. Photo header (~200px) + structured content section. Apple News card pattern. Clean reading order, lowest creative risk, easiest to ship.

Layout 2 — Immersive + Floating Stat. Photo as full background. Three layered floating elements: glass eyebrow chip, glass stat card with the $270 benchmark, social proof avatar cluster ("2,400+ compared this week"), bottom headline. The stat lives in the photo, not under it.

Layout 3 — Story Carousel. Three swipeable panels with Instagram-style progress bars: stat → question → action. Auto-advances every 4s. Photo stays constant, content cycles. Highest engagement surface area on a single card.

Copy decisions

Social proof framing throughout. "Drivers like you in CA" replaces "CA average." More relatable, peer-grounded — Cialdini's social proof works strongest when the reference group feels personal, not aggregated.

The user's actual rate ($310) is intentionally NOT shown on the card. Stating both numbers collapses the curiosity gap — if the comparison is on the card, the user doesn't need to tap. The card poses the question; the destination resolves it. This is the Loewenstein curiosity gap principle.

"Are you overpaying yours?" tightened to "Are you overpaying?" The "yours" is grammatically redundant after the benchmark stat establishes "your auto insurance" as the implied object. Sharper rhythm without losing meaning.

MLDS components used

  • L1: Vertical Banner Card 3.0 (photo region + content region), Button 3.0 Primary Small, Chip 3.0 with glassmorphic backdrop.
  • L2: Vertical Banner Card 3.0 with full-bleed photo, Button 3.0 Primary on imagery, Chip 3.0 (eyebrow + avatar cluster), glass Card with stat. Standard MLDS anatomy on creative backgrounds.
  • L3: Vertical Banner Card 3.0 + internal carousel pattern — not currently in MLDS. Story-style progress bars borrow Progress/Linear visual treatment in a new pagination context.

[System addition] flag for L3

The story carousel is a new pattern. MLDS 4.0 has Tabs and standalone Carousel Banner Cards (single panel each), but no in-card multi-panel pagination component. If we ship L3, this should be formalized as "Story Card 3.0" with: panel container, transition behavior, story-style progress bars, auto-advance timing, pause-on-hover, and tap-to-skip. The pattern is reusable across Marketplace, onboarding, and Cashflow Hub announcements.

Recommendation

For an A/B test, ship L1 vs L2. Both are MLDS-clean and ship-ready. L1 is the safe pattern, L2 is the high-impact pattern. Hypothesis: L2 wins on tap-through (premium imagery + floating data is the pattern interrupt in a feed of mostly-text content), L1 wins on quote completion (clearer expectations set before the tap). L3 is the bigger swing — pursue if there's appetite to add a new MLDS component.
